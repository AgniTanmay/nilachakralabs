AI-Powered Web Analytics Tool for University

Visitor Categorization & Behavioral Data Analysis for Strategic Outreach

About the Research

This research designs an AI-driven web analytics system that extends traditional Google Analytics capabilities for higher education websites. Instead of only reporting past metrics, the system applies machine learning to identify visitor segments, predict conversion likelihood (e.g., inquiry or application), and forecast traffic around key institutional events.

Core Contributions

  • Interaction Quality Measure (IQM): A unified behavioral score combining session duration, pages per session, and event activity to quantify engagement.
  • Visitor Categorization: Unsupervised clustering identifies actionable visitor segments for targeted outreach.
  • Conversion Prediction: Supervised models estimate the probability of desired actions (inquiry, signup), enabling prioritized follow-up.
  • Traffic Forecasting: Time-series models predict visitor volume during admission cycles and events for proactive planning.
  • Privacy-aware Pipeline: Data anonymization and compliance are integrated to protect user privacy while enabling analytics.

Main Motive

To increase student enrollment and improve institutional outreach. By converting passive web analytics into actionable, AI-driven insights, universities can identify high-potential prospects, personalize content, and deploy data-informed recruitment strategies that boost conversions and enrollment.

Why this matters

Traditional analytics show "what happened." This system answers "who is likely to convert," "when interest will peak," and "what content to prioritize" — turning analytics into a strategic decision-support tool for universities.
